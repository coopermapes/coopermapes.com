Corps-vs-Corps Correlation

This correlation heatmap shows how strongly each pair of corps' relative standing moves together, covering 1983–present only (Division I/World Class era). Each corps' standing is relative to the tracked field's yearly average, not raw score. Orange shading means two corps tend to move together (both above or both below the field in the same years); purple means they tend to trade off. Hover a cell for the shared-year count and a 95% confidence interval.

The 7 corps split into two camps — an old guard and a newer wave. Blue Devils, Phantom Regiment, Santa Clara Vanguard, and The Cavaliers move together, carried by strong showings through the '80s, '90s, and 2000s. Bluecoats, Boston Crusaders, and Carolina Crown also move together, on the strength of a competitive surge in the 2010s that's carried into the 2020s.

Average Score by Decade

This bar chart shows each corps' average finals score by decade. The x-axis is decade, the y-axis is finals score in 10-point increments, and each bar is one corps' decade average. A corps with no data for a decade (e.g. Crown in the '70s) isn't given a slot. Crown's 1991–1993 Class A/Div. II years are excluded. Hover a bar for its exact average and year count, or select a corps to see its trend across decades.

The Blue Devils are on track to miss the top decade average for the first time since the 1970s. Bluecoats and Boston Crusaders have both climbed steadily since their inceptions. Only Phantom Regiment and The Cavaliers have posted two decades' worth of decline (Phantom '90s–2010s, Cavs 2000s–2020s). Crown posted the largest decade-over-decade jump of the 21st century — up 6.48 points from the 2000s to the 2010s.

Score Consistency

This boxplot shows score consistency for each corps. The box spans the middle 50% of scores (25th–75th percentile), the line inside it is the median, and the whiskers extend to the most extreme score still within 1.5× the interquartile range. A genuinely anomalous year beyond that range draws as a separate dot instead of stretching the whisker. A tight box means a corps reliably scores in a narrow band year to year; a tall box or long whiskers means real volatility. Y-axis is truncated to 60–100, matching the decade chart, with anything below clipped at the plot edge. Crown's 1991–1993 Class A/Div. II years are excluded. Hover any part of a box for its exact numbers.
